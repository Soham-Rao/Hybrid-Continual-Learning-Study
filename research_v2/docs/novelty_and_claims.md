# Novelty And Claims v2

## Desired Positioning
The project should be presented as:
- a controlled comparative benchmark of continual-learning baselines and hybrids
- a study of mechanism-level behavior through ablations
- a practical decision-support layer for method selection under memory/compute constraints

## Primary v2 Claim Areas
### Experimental novelty
- apples-to-apples comparison of baselines and hybrids in one framework
- same training harness, metrics, logging, and evaluation across methods
- same local-machine execution strategy across the four core datasets

### Method novelty
- A-GEM+Distill
- SI-DER

### Analysis novelty
- component-level hybrid ablations
- trade-off analysis across accuracy, forgetting, memory, and runtime
- recommendation outputs grounded in empirical results rather than intuition alone

## Claim Discipline
Safe claim style for v2:
- controlled benchmark
- original hybrid combinations in the framework
- systematic ablation-driven analysis
- practical recommendation layer based on evidence

Claims that require especially strong evidence before use:
- best overall method across all datasets
- universally superior hybrid
- publication-strength recommendation policy
- any SOTA-style claim

## v2 Standard For Claims
A claim is only allowed in the final project if:
- it is backed by v2 reruns
- it uses the final intended seed count
- it is consistent across the corresponding plots/tables/stats
- it survives the ablation and caveat review
